# Website Strategy Notes

## Research-driven structure

The page was rebuilt around a fast-read B2B SaaS landing page structure:

1. Clear hero value proposition in the first screen.
2. Immediate CTA and video access.
3. Problem framing before feature lists.
4. Product proof through screenshots.
5. Outcome-led feature copy instead of generic feature names.
6. Strong mobile and accessibility support.

## Competitive positioning

Competitors in SOP/training software emphasize:

- AI-assisted SOP/process creation.
- Centralized training and onboarding.
- Accountability and completion tracking.
- Mobile-friendly access.
- QR code access at the point of work.

The new page positions TechSavvy SOP Portal as a practical, branded system for small businesses that need AI-assisted setup, QR-powered mobility, training, checklists, change requests, and manager control.

## Messaging choices

Hero headline:

> Stop answering the same process questions all day.

Reason: speaks directly to the manager/owner pain instead of opening with a generic product label.

Primary value proposition:

> TechSavvy SOP Portal turns scattered instructions into one searchable, branded system with AI-assisted SOP drafts, QR-code mobile access, training checks, checklist runs, and manager approval controls.

Reason: says what it is, who it helps, and why it matters in one readable block.

## Current limitations

- Form submission needs a production form endpoint.
- Pricing/eligibility language should be confirmed before publishing.
- Real testimonials, measured outcomes, and customer proof should be added when approved.
